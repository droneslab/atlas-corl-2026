Overview

ATLAS aims to build a forum around active image formation, multimodal sensing control, and learning methods that convert better observations into better embodied behavior.

Active perception in robotics has traditionally emphasized viewpoint selection and sensor placement, while computational photography has shown that image formation itself can be controlled through exposure, illumination, coding, and other acquisition choices. This workshop asks how robot learning and generative models can unify these ideas: enabling robots to predict counterfactual observations under alternative sensing actions, optimize how data is acquired, and improve downstream performance in navigation, SLAM, manipulation, and exploration.

Scope

We aim to bring together researchers across robot learning, active perception, generative modelling, computational photography, and computational imaging.

  • Active image formation for robot perception and control
  • Exposure, gain, HDR, and illumination control
  • Flash, structured light, coded sensing, and adaptive acquisition
  • Multimodal sensing and active selection of sensing parameters
  • Relighting, rendering, and counterfactual observation models
  • World models for sensing choices and information gain
  • Task-grounded evaluation in navigation, SLAM, manipulation, exploration, and safety-critical settings
  • Hardware-software co-design for embodied perception

Program

The workshop is planned as a full-day program built around invited talks and poster sessions, with coffee breaks in the morning and afternoon and a lunch break at midday.

Exact timing may still be refined, but the day will follow this invited-talks and posters template.

Call for Papers

We invite concise 4 to 6 page papers on research directions that fit the workshop scope.

Contributions may cover new methods, systems, datasets, or evaluations in active perception, generative modeling, and computational imaging for robotics.

Use the topics above as a guide for submissions that align with the workshop themes.
